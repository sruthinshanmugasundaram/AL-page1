Welcome to Our Site

Welcome to the Transforming India Initiative. Launched in 2016, our mission is to empower the nation’s youth through comprehensive programs designed to equip them with essential skills and knowledge. Our aim is to nurture a generation of change-makers capable of making a meaningful impact on India’s socio-economic landscape. Through initiatives like the Social Entrepreneurship Program (SEP) and Reimagine Responsible Enterprises (RRE) Fellowship, we provide young individuals with the tools and mindset to become leaders and entrepreneurs, fostering innovation and social responsibility. Join us in shaping the future of the development sector and inspiring positive change in our communities.

About Access Livelihoods

We established Access Livelihoods in 2005 in our quest to find effective ways to solve the persistent problems faced in the livelihoods of economically marginalized communities. Our work across 26 states in India over the last seventeen years has helped us create and test new livelihood models that transform producers' lives and usher greater self-reliance. Through our Beehive model for building producer enterprises, we presently partner over 75,000 farmers, weavers, and tribal farmers in building 68 producer companies in five states of India. We also work at the macro level, with innovative platforms that seek to shape the livelihood environment.

Livelihood Models that Transform the Producer Ecosystem

  • Beehive: Focuses on building producer enterprises to enhance self-reliance among farmers, weavers, and tribal farmers.
  • Collective Enterprise (Cross Pollination): Sustains non-profits by promoting collaboration and resource-sharing.
  • Reviving Cooperatives (Metamorphosis): Helps to rejuvenate and modernize traditional cooperative structures.
  • Corporate-Producer Co-evolution (Symbiosis): Encourages partnerships between corporate entities and producer companies.
  • Networked Enterprises (Web): Builds a network of interconnected enterprises to strengthen the producer ecosystem.
